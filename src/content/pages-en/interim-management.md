## When interim management is the right answer

Some situations cannot be managed with internal resources or an overhead consulting firm. They require a senior executive who is committed to execution.

You need an interim manager when:

- **A leadership role is vacant** and operational continuity is at stake — every week without a pilot costs.
- **A transformation is drifting** — budget, timelines, results — and teams need firm framing to get back on track.
- **You are preparing a Series B** and your processes will not hold the scale-up without restructuring first.
- **A business unit is in difficulty** — accelerating churn, collapsing margins, tightening cash.
- **You are integrating AI into your operations** and projects keep ending as POCs with no follow-through for lack of governance.
- **A strategic project is starting** — market launch, M&A, delivery overhaul — and you need an experienced orchestrator.

In all these situations, interim management gives you immediate access to an executive with directly applicable experience, for a defined duration.

---

## What interim management is not

Interim management is not consulting. It is not coaching or mentoring either.

It is an operational appointment. The interim manager takes a role — CEO, COO, CFO, CDO, CIO, or strategic project lead — and exercises it for real, with the teams, day to day.

They do not produce a report. They lead.

## Frequently asked questions

**What is the minimum duration for an interim management assignment?**
Assignments typically start at three months. Below that, it is difficult to scope properly, execute, and leave a stable organisation behind. The standard duration is 6 to 12 months, with an extension possible if the situation requires it.

**How does an assignment start?**
The first week is dedicated to diagnosis and scoping: understanding the challenges, auditing teams and processes, defining mission KPIs. The first levers are activated by day 15–30. A formal review with the board or shareholders takes place at day 60.

**What does an interim management assignment cost?**
Assignments are billed on a daily rate or fixed fee depending on the nature of the engagement. There is no public pricing — each assignment is scoped individually based on the challenges, duration, and scope.

**Interim manager or permanent hire: which to choose?**
If you need to close a gap quickly — ongoing transformation, vacant leadership role, critical project — and the need is time-bounded, interim management is the answer. If you have six months ahead of you, a stable situation, and a permanent need, a permanent hire is more appropriate. In crisis or transformation situations, the two timelines are not comparable.

**Can you work outside France?**
Yes. Past assignments have included deployments in West Africa, Europe, and the Middle East. The working language is adapted to the context.

**How is ROI measured?**
KPIs are defined at the initial scoping stage. They are tracked monthly. At the end of the assignment, every result is documented and traceable. ROI is measurable — that is a requirement, not an aspiration.
