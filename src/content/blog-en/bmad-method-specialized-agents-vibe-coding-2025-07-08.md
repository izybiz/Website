# BMAD method: a team of specialised agents so that AI delivers what you actually specified

You described what you wanted. The AI agent produced something. It is not quite what you had in mind, but it resembles it. You re-prompt. Things change, but something else breaks. Three iterations later, you have code that runs and a feature that does not quite do what you intended.

That is vibe coding. And most of your teams are doing exactly this right now.

The BMAD method — released in May 2025 — solves this problem at the root. Rather than throwing a prompt at an AI agent and hoping for a coherent result, it enforces a structured AI development workflow: a team of specialised agents — analyst, PM, architect, developer, QA — who pass structured artefacts to one another, ensuring the AI builds what you actually specified.

---

## The problem is not AI. It is the absence of structure.

When a human developer receives a vague brief, they ask questions, reframe the problem, request examples. They fill the grey areas with judgement.

An AI agent does the opposite: it fills grey areas with assumptions. Quickly, without friction, without signalling that it is improvising. The result is plausible. It is not necessarily correct.

Vibe coding exploits this speed. It pays for it in coherence. Each successive prompt optimises locally, with no overarching vision. Architecture fragments. Business logic gets lost. The code produced is difficult to understand, difficult to test, and difficult to evolve.

This is not a problem with the AI model. It is a problem with the governance of the development workflow. Without AI development governance, every project becomes a gamble.

---

## What the BMAD method actually is

The BMAD method (Breakthrough Method for Agile AI-Driven Development) is an open-source agentic framework that orchestrates AI development around two principles: agent specialisation and artefact structuring.

**Agent specialisation.** The BMAD method does not entrust development to a generalist agent. It defines five distinct roles in the AI agent orchestration, each with a precise scope and its own way of working:

- **The analyst** transforms a raw business need into structured requirements. They ask the right questions, identify grey areas, and produce a complete requirements document.
- **The PM** (Product Manager) takes those requirements and translates them into prioritised user stories, with explicit acceptance criteria for each feature.
- **The architect** designs the technical structure: modules, interfaces, dependencies, constraints. They produce an architecture plan that the developer can follow without improvising.
- **The developer** implements from the architect's plan and the PM's user stories. They do not fill grey areas, they flag them.
- **The QA** validates that the implementation matches the acceptance criteria defined by the PM. Not what seems to work, what was specified.

**Artefact structuring.** Each agent produces a structured document that becomes the input for the next. The analyst's requirements document feeds the PM. The architect's plan feeds the developer. The PM's acceptance criteria feed the QA.

Nothing is lost in a chain of prompts. Everything is documented, versionable, auditable. This is what separates the BMAD method from improvised AI agent orchestration.

## What the BMAD method changes for business leaders

Most executives who see the BMAD method for the first time ask the same question: "But who writes the initial brief? Where does it all start?"

The answer: you. Or your product director. Or your head of operations.

The BMAD analyst does not start from code. They start from a business need expressed in plain language. What you want to build, for whom, why, and with what constraints. They structure that need, ask clarifying questions, and produce the document that feeds the chain.

The critical skill is no longer knowing how to code. It is knowing how to express a need precisely. And that is exactly what business leaders and executives have always done, under different formats, with different names.

**What the BMAD framework shifts:**

- The senior developer no longer spends their time interpreting vague requirements. They validate that the implementation conforms to the architecture.
- The project manager no longer serves as an intermediary between the business and the technical team. The structured artefacts fill that role.
- Validation back-and-forth decreases because acceptance criteria are defined before the first line of code is written.

The operations director of a 200-person industrial SME implemented the BMAD method on a preventive maintenance management tool at the start of 2025. Initial brief written in two hours. The BMAD analyst produced a requirements document in half a day. First functional module delivered: three and a half weeks. Alignment with the original brief: 91%.

"Before, we would go through three scoping meetings before starting. And at the end, we would still get something that only matched about 70% of what we had described. This time, I wrote what I wanted once. And that is what was delivered."

## What the BMAD method is not

**It is not a turnkey solution.** The BMAD framework is open source. It requires configuration, adaptation to the project context, and a technical profile capable of orchestrating the specialised AI agents. This is not a tool you install in an hour.

**It is not suited to every project.** A simple internal tool, a well-bounded module, a process automation: the BMAD method would be over-engineered for these. Its value emerges on projects with multiple modules, technical dependencies, and a need for coherence over time.

**It is not a way to eliminate developers.** It is a way to reposition them. A senior developer who validates structured artefacts and orchestrates specialised agents delivers more value than ten developers each interpreting vague requirements in their own way. The agentic framework amplifies expertise — it does not replace it.

## How to adopt the BMAD method without getting it wrong

**Choose the right first project.** Not too simple (you will not see the value) and not too complex (you will not manage to configure the BMAD method and run the project simultaneously). A project with three to five modules, a functional scope you can describe in an hour, and a clear business sponsor.

**Train an orchestrator.** The BMAD method requires a technical profile who understands how the agents interact and how to intervene when an artefact is incomplete or poorly oriented. This does not have to be your CTO — it is someone who has mastered the AI methodological framework and can drive it on your first project.

**Measure functional alignment.** Not just delivery timelines. Compare what you asked for (the PM's acceptance criteria) against what was delivered. That is the metric that validates whether the BMAD method worked as intended. **Document the artefacts from the first project.** The documents produced by the BMAD agents on your first project become your templates. They accelerate the second project, then the third. Structured AI development compounds over time.

## What the BMAD method is concretely worth

The BMAD method does not solve the problem of generative AI. It solves the problem of AI development governance. It imposes structure where vibe coding leaves room for improvisation. It ensures that each agent works from a structured artefact produced by the previous one, not from an interpretation of a vague prompt.

The result: code that matches what you specified. Code that is maintainable because the artefacts document its logic. Code that is auditable because every decision made by every agent is traced within a structured development workflow.

This is not an AI promise. It is a method. And methods are meant to be managed.
